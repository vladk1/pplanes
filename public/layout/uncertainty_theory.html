Theory behind estimation

The situation, looks like this: you have two planes with different properties and you need to play one of them. Probably you have no idea on how difficult it is to build any of the planes or how would they perfom. Or maybe you can say "it couldn't be less than this and more than this". In fact, this is how you deal with uncertainty, you use estimation intervals. But what can you do with this?

We use a Bayesian Cost Benefit Analysis inspired tool which computes Monte Carlo simulation over each interval of estimation given by you - building time and flight distance.

The intervals are treated as 95% confidence intervals with normal distributions. Some of the values would be outside the interval. Therefore, when you do the estimation, you need to have a pretty confident estimation interval.

From the sampled data, the tool computes the expected value of each interval. This is represented by the mean value of the samples. Applying the rules of the game, it translates the measurements from meters and seconds to points. This gives you the Expected Cost and the Expected Benefit.

Then, it calculates the Expected Net Benefit, Value at Risk and Loss Probability.
