How many flights are from London to Paris every day?*

There is more than one possible answer to this question. To reduce this uncertainty, we approximate an interval.

A scientific approach would be to use a 95% Confidence Interval. There will be 2.5% chance that the true answer is below the lower bound and 2.5% chance that it is above the upper bound.

Good approximation of the probability distribution can be computed through Monte Carlo simulation. From this, one can compute the expected answer.

Uncertainty is important in the early stages of software development when an organisation makes strategic decisions about which projects to fund or when software architects make tradeoff design decisions. These decisions aim at maximising the benefits, subject to costs and time constraints.

*around 35-40 planes
