This is what the Bayesian Cost Benefit Analysis tells of your data:
ECost or Expected Cost, is the mean value of sampling the estimated cost
EBenefit or Expected Benefit, is the mean value of sampling the estimated benefit
ENB is the Excepted Net Benefit, which is indeed the Expected Benefit - Expected Cost
LP is the Loss Probability, which reflects the probability to lose the value at risk
VaR is the Value at Risk and represents how much value you might lose in this scenario

Take a note of what the results are. You will have to make a decision next!
