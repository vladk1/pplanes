Monte Carlo methods are a broad class of computational algorithms that rely on repeated random sampling to obtain numerical results. The method simulates input randomly from a probability distribution over the domain. It is useful when problems are difficult or impossible to solve by other methods. However it only simulates, but not analyzes.

The method requires estimate values for both cost and benefits, including its maximum, minimum and likely values. Then the system can simulate cases as much as we want with a given type of distribution.

In this case, users estimate the values for minimum cost, likely cost, maximum cost, minimum benefit, likely benefit, maximum benefit and regard both of cost and benefit as a triangular distribution.
