More on the decision-making tool:

To approximate the distribution for cost and benefit we can use Monte Carlo simulation.

The underlying principle is to sample a large number of simulation scenarios generated by probability distribution and estimated values.

From the result of a Monte Carlo simulation, one can estimate measures of interest to decision makers such as the expected net benefit (ENB), loss probability (LP) and value at risk (VaR), defined as follow:

Expected Net Benefit is the overall outcome of cost-benefit analysis, that treats the stream of costs and benefits in a net value calculation. Generally, this is the value that we want to maximize.

Risk is measured by the loss probability and the value at risk.

Value at Risk (VaR) summarizes the potential risks of failure by a single number. We can say that, VaR is the amount of benefits a portfolio can loss within a particular scenario and at a given confidence interval.
